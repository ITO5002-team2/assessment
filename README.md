
#Idea

Vaccination Coverage and Awareness Dashboard for parents and carers of young children in Australia. It supports SDG 3: Good Health and Well-Being by making childhood immunisation data easier to understand and use. Australia’s official data shows childhood coverage is below ideal levels in some age groups and areas, so this feels like a relevant and realistic project.

#Data Sources

· Australian Government – Childhood immunisation coverage: https://www.health.gov.au/topics/immunisation/immunisation-data/childhood-immunisation-coverage

· ABS – Regional population by age and sex: https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/people/population/regional-population-age-and-sex/latest-release

#Key Stakeholders

Parents and carers, public health agencies, community health organisations, local councils, and immunisation providers.

#Concerns/Problems / Why they are important?

Childhood vaccination is one of the most important preventive health measures, but coverage is not uniform across Australia. Some children and communities remain more vulnerable when coverage is lower, and parents may find official data hard to interpret. That makes this both a public health issue and an information-access issue.

#What do we intend to do and how will it help the rationale behind it

The plan would be to build a simple dashboard that shows vaccination coverage by area, compares regions, highlights lower-coverage locations, and overlays child population data. The rationale is that it turns official public data into something clearer and more useful for families and communities
